What We Do

Four Pillars of Our Work

View All Areas

Research

We conduct independent, rigorous research on climate change, fossil fuel dependency, renewable energy, and the political economy of Pakistan's energy sector to inform evidence-based policymaking.

Read More

Collaboration

We build networks of researchers, civil society, government actors, and international institutions to foster cross-sector partnerships that amplify collective action on climate and development.

Read More

Advocacy

We translate research into actionable policy recommendations, engaging legislators, regulators, and international climate bodies to champion equitable and just energy transition pathways for Pakistan.

Read More

Outreach

We engage communities, media, and the public to build climate awareness and grassroots demand for equitable energy policies, ensuring that the voices of affected populations are central to the discourse.

Read More

Our Mission

Driving Equitable
Energy Transition

To accelerate Pakistan's transition from fossil fuels to renewable energy while ensuring that this transition is just, equitable, and inclusive — particularly for low-income and vulnerable communities. We believe that climate action must go hand-in-hand with social justice and economic equity.

Our Approach

How We Create Lasting Impact

01

Evidence-Based Research

Our multidisciplinary research teams produce policy briefs, energy monitors, and in-depth studies that ground climate advocacy in rigorous data. We combine quantitative modelling with qualitative fieldwork to capture Pakistan's complex energy landscape.

Explore Publications
02

Policy Engagement & Advocacy

From parliamentary briefings to international climate negotiations, PRIED actively engages decision-makers with practical, actionable policy recommendations. We provide technical assistance and prepare position papers that directly influence legislation and regulation.
